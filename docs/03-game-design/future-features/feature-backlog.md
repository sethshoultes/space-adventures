# Feature Ideas Backlog

**Status:** Future Consideration
**Last Updated:** 2024

This document contains promising feature ideas for Space Adventures that are not currently prioritized for MVP or immediate Post-MVP development. These ideas remain available for future phases or expansion packs.

## Overview

The following six features showed potential but were not selected for priority development. They are documented here for:
- Future reference and consideration
- Community feedback and voting
- Potential expansion pack content
- Post-Phase 2 development

## Backlog Ideas

---

### 1. Salvage Economy & Faction System

**Concept:** Multiple salvage factions compete on Earth with different philosophies on rebuilding vs. leaving

**Priority:** Medium
**Complexity:** High
**Phase:** Phase 1 Extension or DLC

#### Summary

Create competing salvage factions on Earth, each with their own ideology, territory, and resources. Players can join factions, play them against each other, or remain independent. Faction choices affect available missions, parts, and the society left behind when you launch.

#### Core Mechanics

**Factions:**
1. **The Traders** - Pragmatic merchants, value profit and efficiency
2. **The Scientists** - Academic survivors, value knowledge and research
3. **The Militarists** - Ex-military, value order and security
4. **The Idealists** - Utopian dreamers, value community and cooperation

**Reputation System:**
- Reputation with each faction (0-100)
- Faction-specific missions
- Exclusive parts and technology
- Political maneuvering between factions

**Player Impact:**
- Broker peace or fuel conflicts
- Unite factions or exploit divisions
- Shape Earth's remaining society
- Your choices determine who leads after you leave

#### Why Not Prioritized

- Adds significant complexity to Phase 1
- May distract from core ship-building loop
- Faction politics might overshadow narrative
- Requires substantial content creation (4+ faction questlines)

#### Implementation Estimate

- **Time:** 4-6 weeks
- **Complexity:** High (politics, branching consequences)
- **Content:** 30-50 faction missions
- **Systems:** Reputation, faction AI, territory control

#### Future Potential

- Strong candidate for "Phase 1.5" expansion
- Could add 10-15 hours of additional gameplay
- Provides replayability through faction choices
- Natural fit for "Director's Cut" version

### 2. Environmental Hazards & System Dependencies

**Concept:** Ship systems dynamically affect moment-to-moment gameplay through environmental challenges

**Priority:** Medium-High
**Complexity:** Medium
**Phase:** Phase 2 Extension

#### Summary

Create environmental hazards in space that require specific system combinations to overcome. Radiation zones need Shields + Life Support. Dense nebulae need Sensors + Computer Core. System damage cascades and creates engineering puzzles during crises.

#### Core Mechanics

**Hazard Types:**
- **Radiation Zones** - Require Shields + Life Support
- **Dense Nebulae** - Require Sensors + Computer Core for navigation
- **Gravity Wells** - Require Propulsion + Warp coordination
- **Electrical Storms** - Require Shields + Power management
- **Asteroid Fields** - Require Sensors + Propulsion

**System Dependencies:**
- Systems work together synergistically
- Damage to one system affects related systems
- Emergency protocols when critical systems fail
- "Engineering puzzle" mini-game during crises

**Dynamic Events:**
- Random encounters with hazards
- Time-pressure scenarios
- Resource management (power allocation)
- Crew skill checks to compensate for damaged systems

#### Why Not Prioritized

- Systems already have function (power, level requirements)
- May add tedious management over fun gameplay
- Risk of frustrating players with cascading failures
- Core ship systems already complex enough for MVP

#### Implementation Estimate

- **Time:** 3-4 weeks
- **Complexity:** Medium (system interactions, balance)
- **Content:** 10-15 hazard types, emergency scenarios
- **Systems:** Hazard detection, cascade logic, emergency protocols

#### Future Potential

- Good "hard mode" or difficulty setting
- Potential for "Simulation Mode" DLC
- Could appeal to engineering/puzzle fans
- Natural fit for post-launch expansion

### 3. Anomaly Investigation System

**Concept:** Procedural science mini-game for discovering spatial anomalies and phenomena

**Priority:** Medium
**Complexity:** Medium
**Phase:** Phase 2 Extension

#### Summary

Discover spatial anomalies, alien artifacts, and scientific phenomena during exploration. Players investigate using Science skill + Sensors, with multiple approaches (scan, probe, direct contact). Risk/reward system with unique upgrades and lore as rewards.

#### Core Mechanics

**Anomaly Types:**
- Spatial phenomena (wormholes, subspace rifts)
- Alien artifacts (ancient technology)
- Energy beings (sentient anomalies)
- Temporal distortions
- Dark matter concentrations

**Investigation Approaches:**
- **Scan from Distance** - Safe but limited data
- **Deploy Probe** - Better data, lose probe if dangerous
- **Direct Contact** - Maximum data, maximum risk
- **Specialized Analysis** - Requires high Science/Sensors

**Rewards:**
- Unique ship upgrades
- Scientific journal entries
- Lore discoveries
- Technology unlocks
- Possible dangers

**Scientific Journal:**
- Track all discoveries
- Build knowledge database
- Share findings with alien species
- Unlock special missions

#### Why Not Prioritized

- Core exploration loop already functional
- Could feel repetitive without sufficient variety
- Competes with mission content for player time
- Requires significant procedural generation work

#### Implementation Estimate

- **Time:** 3-4 weeks
- **Complexity:** Medium (procedural generation, variety)
- **Content:** 20-30 anomaly types, investigation paths
- **Systems:** Anomaly generation, investigation mechanics, journal

#### Future Potential

- Great "Science Vessel" specialization content
- Could be free update to add variety
- Community could submit anomaly ideas
- Natural fit for ongoing content updates

### 4. Ship Classification Specialization Perks

**Concept:** Expand existing ship classification system with gameplay-affecting unique abilities

**Priority:** Low-Medium
**Complexity:** Low
**Phase:** Post-MVP Quick Win

#### Summary

Build on the existing 10-ship classification system by adding unique abilities and bonuses for each class. Science Vessels get bonus science mission rewards, Explorers reveal hidden locations, Combat Vessels get intimidation bonuses, etc.

#### Core Mechanics

**Ship Class Perks:**

**Science Vessel:**
- +25% science mission rewards
- Extra anomaly spawn rate
- Improved sensor range
- Special science missions unlocked

**Explorer:**
- Reveals hidden locations on sector maps
- +15% first contact success
- Reduced fuel consumption
- Discovers shortcuts

**Combat Vessel:**
- Intimidation in negotiations
- +20% combat mission success
- Enhanced weapon efficiency
- Enemy ships less likely to attack

**Support Vessel:**
- Can assist other ships/NPCs
- Reputation bonuses with all factions
- Special humanitarian missions
- Crew morale bonus

**Courier:**
- Faster travel between locations
- Trade mission bonuses
- Special delivery missions
- Black market access

**Dreadnought:**
- Massive intimidation factor
- Can overwhelm defenses
- Flagship missions
- Commands respect

*[6 more classes with unique perks]*

**Respec Option:**
- Rebuild ship for different class
- Costs resources
- Allows playstyle experimentation
- Achievement for mastering all classes

#### Why Not Prioritized

- Ship classification already exists (from ship-classification-system.md)
- Perks could unbalance gameplay
- May force players into specific builds
- Better to get core gameplay right first

#### Implementation Estimate

- **Time:** 1-2 weeks
- **Complexity:** Low (mostly stat bonuses and flags)
- **Content:** 10 ship classes × 3-5 perks each
- **Systems:** Perk application, respec mechanics

#### Future Potential

- **Excellent post-launch patch content**
- Easy to balance over time
- Community can suggest perks
- Natural progression from existing system
- **Recommended for Phase 2, Week 10+**

### 5. Temporal Anomalies & Time Loop Missions

**Concept:** Special missions involving time distortion with Groundhog Day-style puzzle solving

**Priority:** Low
**Complexity:** High
**Phase:** Special Content / DLC

#### Summary

Certain missions can be "reset" with player knowledge retained, creating Groundhog Day-style puzzle scenarios. Players learn what to do through repetition, make choices about using time loop knowledge, and face consequences for tampering with time.

#### Core Mechanics

**Time Loop Missions:**
- Mission resets but player remembers
- Learn patrol patterns, dialogue, outcomes
- Use knowledge to achieve "perfect" outcome
- Ethical choice: Use foreknowledge or not?

**Example Mission: "The Station Disaster":**
1. **Loop 1:** Station explodes, crew dies, you escape
2. **Loop 2:** You try to warn them, they don't believe you
3. **Loop 3:** You find evidence of sabotage
4. **Loop 4:** You prevent sabotage, but attacker kills someone
5. **Loop 5:** You save everyone (if you figured it out)

**Consequences:**
- Some loops, you can't save everyone
- Moral weight of choosing who lives/dies
- NPC reactions to your "prescience"
- Possible corruption from temporal knowledge

**Link to Mystery:**
- Why are there temporal anomalies?
- Related to Exodus fleet somehow?
- Bigger temporal threat?

#### Why Not Prioritized

- Extremely complex to implement correctly
- Requires extensive branching and state tracking
- May confuse or frustrate players
- Time travel adds narrative complexity
- Better suited to standalone special content

#### Implementation Estimate

- **Time:** 5-8 weeks (very complex)
- **Complexity:** Very High (state management, branching)
- **Content:** 3-5 time loop missions with deep branching
- **Systems:** Temporal mechanics, memory persistence, branching

#### Future Potential

- Perfect for special DLC: "Temporal Anomalies"
- Could be signature "mind-bending" content
- Community would debate optimal solutions
- Memorable, unique gameplay experience
- **Recommend as standalone expansion if resources allow**

### 6. Legacy & New Game+

**Concept:** Choices echo across playthroughs with meta-narrative about cycles of civilization

**Priority:** Low
**Complexity:** Medium
**Phase:** Post-Launch / Replayability Update

#### Summary

New Game+ mode where major story choices create "historical markers" that affect future playthroughs. Your previous ship can be discovered as derelict wreckage. Different Exodus scenarios based on previous endings. Meta-narrative about cycles of civilization.

#### Core Mechanics

**New Game+ Features:**
- Start with small bonuses (5% XP, starting resources)
- "Historical markers" from previous playthrough appear
- Can discover your old ship as derelict
- Different Exodus scenarios each playthrough

**Legacy System:**
- Major choices become "history" in new game
- Example: If you helped Faction A, they're stronger in NG+
- If you found certain evidence, it's public knowledge in NG+
- Your ending affects starting conditions

**Meta-Narrative:**
- Multiple cycles of human space exploration
- Pattern of rise and fall
- Philosophical questions about progress
- Optional "true ending" after multiple playthroughs

**Unlockables:**
- New ship classes
- Secret missions
- Alternative story paths
- Harder difficulties

#### Why Not Prioritized

- Requires complete game first
- Most players won't replay immediately
- Development resources better spent on initial experience
- Can be added post-launch

#### Implementation Estimate

- **Time:** 2-3 weeks
- **Complexity:** Medium (save file parsing, legacy logic)
- **Content:** New Game+ missions, historical variations
- **Systems:** Legacy persistence, NG+ bonuses, meta-story

#### Future Potential

- **Excellent post-launch content update**
- Encourages replays and player retention
- Low cost, high player value
- Could drive second wave of interest
- **Recommended for 3-6 months post-launch**

## Backlog Prioritization Framework

When considering backlog features for development:

### Evaluation Criteria

1. **Player Value** - How much does this enhance the experience?
2. **Implementation Cost** - Time and complexity
3. **Risk** - Could this break existing systems or confuse players?
4. **Timing** - Is this right for current development phase?
5. **Alignment** - Does it fit the Star Trek TNG serious sci-fi tone?

### Recommendation Tiers

**Tier 1 - Strong Future Candidates:**
- Ship Classification Specialization Perks (quick win, builds on existing)
- Legacy & New Game+ (post-launch retention)

**Tier 2 - Good Expansion Content:**
- Salvage Economy & Faction System (Phase 1.5 expansion)
- Anomaly Investigation System (Science Vessel expansion)

**Tier 3 - Specialist/DLC Content:**
- Environmental Hazards & System Dependencies (Simulation Mode DLC)
- Temporal Anomalies & Time Loop Missions (Special narrative DLC)
